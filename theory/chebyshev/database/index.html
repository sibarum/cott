1. Abstract

We have shown how Chebyshev polynomials emerge from totalized base-zero exponentiation. In this article we discuss the software architecture of solving any-dimensional, any-basis, discrete transformations on traction tensors. The information-conservative traction tensor is surjective to ℂ. This technique uses algebraic rules, logarithmic decomposition, and canonical forms for caching known equivalences.

---
2. Architecture

Traction tensors are defined as polynomials of traction terms.

The dyadic-basis tensor is derived from sqrt(-1).

sqrt(-1) = 0^{w/2}
C(0^{w/2}) => i

Non-dyadic basis tensors are chosen for primality. Given p is some prime positive integer:

C(0^{w*p}) => e^(i*pi*p)

Exact basis mappings are saved in an in-memory database whenever they are encountered for the first time.

0^{w*u}
C(0^{w*u})
0^{w/2}
i
0^{w/3}
{1/2}+sqrt(3)*i/2
0^{w/5}
{1+sqrt(5)}/4+i*{sqrt((5-sqrt(5))/8)}

Simplified Chebyshev recurrences are likewise cached.

Decomposition metadata would be included alongside computed values, for ease of indexing. For example:
